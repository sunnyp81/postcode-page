Use this checklist before making an offer on any property. The data checks take 30–45 minutes. The site visit takes a few hours. Together they can save you from buying into the wrong area — or help you buy confidently into the right one.

## Online Data Checks

### House Prices
- [ ] Look up the postcode district's current average price on [postcode.page](/) — is it what you expected?
- [ ] Check the 1-year price change — rising, flat, or falling?
- [ ] Check the 5-year history — consistent growth, volatility, or a recent peak?
- [ ] Check price by property type — how does your target type sit vs the average?
- [ ] Look up the specific postcode sector for micro-level price context

### Crime
- [ ] Check the district's crime rate per 1,000 residents vs the national average
- [ ] Look at the crime breakdown — burglary and vehicle crime are most relevant to homeowners
- [ ] Check whether there is a 12-month trend (rising or falling)

### Schools
- [ ] Count Outstanding schools in the district — are there any?
- [ ] Check the % Good/Outstanding — above 70% is solid, above 85% is strong
- [ ] For specific schools, check inspection date — ratings older than 5 years are less reliable
- [ ] Verify catchment areas with the local authority admissions portal

### Council Tax
- [ ] Find the Band D rate for the local authority on [postcode.page](/)
- [ ] Identify the band of the specific property via [VOA](https://www.gov.uk/council-tax-bands)
- [ ] Calculate the actual annual bill (band fraction × Band D rate)
- [ ] Compare to your current bill if moving from another area

### Broadband
- [ ] Check the district's average download speed and full-fibre coverage % on [postcode.page](/)
- [ ] Run a property-level check with [Openreach](https://www.openreach.com/fibre-broadband/fttp-network-checker) or your preferred ISP
- [ ] If working from home, verify minimum speed for video calls (30 Mbps+)

### Other Checks
- [ ] Check flood risk at [check-long-term-flood-risk.service.gov.uk](https://check-long-term-flood-risk.service.gov.uk/)
- [ ] Check planning applications near the property via the local authority planning portal
- [ ] Check Rightmove/Zoopla history — how many times has this property been listed?

## Comparing Areas

- [ ] If deciding between two districts, use the [postcode.page comparison tool](/compare/)
- [ ] Weight factors based on your priorities (schools, crime, price trend, commute)
- [ ] Check total cost of ownership: mortgage + council tax + commute costs for each area

## Site Visit Checklist

**Timing**
- [ ] Visit on a weekday during commuting hours (7–9am or 5–7pm)
- [ ] Visit on a Friday or Saturday evening
- [ ] Visit on a Sunday morning

**Street**
- [ ] Condition of neighbouring properties (gardens, paintwork, windows)
- [ ] Parking availability at different times
- [ ] Traffic noise levels
- [ ] Proximity to HGV routes, railways, flight paths
- [ ] Any unusual smells or noise sources

**Neighbourhood**
- [ ] Quality of local high street / shops
- [ ] Nearest GP surgery and dentist
- [ ] Parks and green space
- [ ] Pubs, cafés, restaurants — are they busy?
- [ ] Demographic feel — does it match what you are looking for?

## Questions to Ask the Estate Agent

- Why is the vendor selling?
- How long has the property been on the market? Has the price been reduced?
- Have any offers previously fallen through, and why?
- What is the council tax band?
- When was the boiler last serviced?
- Has the property ever flooded or had damp issues?
- What are the parking arrangements?
- Are there any known disputes with neighbours?

## Legal and Financial Checks (via Solicitor)

- [ ] Local authority search (planning, highways, drainage)
- [ ] Environmental search (contamination, flooding, subsidence)
- [ ] Drainage and water search
- [ ] Title deeds and boundaries
- [ ] For leasehold: remaining lease length, service charge history, ground rent terms
- [ ] For shared ownership: staircasing terms, restrictions
